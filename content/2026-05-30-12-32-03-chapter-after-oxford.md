# Chapter After Oxford

Oxford University lived up to every expectation I had; beautiful old building, bizarre traditions
and incredibly intelligent students and lecturers. Here I offer some reflections on my masters year
attending the university, as well as life in Oxford outside of the libraries. Finally, I have a
discussion on the next stage of my life doing a PhD at the University of Cape Town (UCT).

## Oxford Academics

I did the
[Masters in Mathematical Sciences at Oxford](https://www.ox.ac.uk/admissions/graduate/courses/msc-mathematical-sciences),
referred to as OMMS. The program offers a modules covering a huge selection of mathematics topics. I
found this program very difficult to two main reasons:

1. I was focusing on applied mathematics. Meaning that I was doing lots of work with ODE's and
   PDE's. The issue is that I have a pure maths background. So, getting the intuition for deriving a
   PDE to describe a river flowing was difficult but I was not about the methods that one then uses
   to assess the stability of the model or to solve the model. This meant I felt like I was always
   on the back foot – needing to learn the methods and applications at the same time.
2. Oxford has a very high standard of examination. Far more difficult than the exams that I have
   faced in South Africa. Generally, as a baseline if I attended lectures and did the tutorials at
   UCT then I could pass a module. But at Oxford, doing this was just not enough. Lectures were very
   fast paced. If you were not reading before and after a lecture and spending days on problem
   sheet, asking questions I think it would be very difficult to pass. This is definitely conflated
   with the first point but nevertheless they are difficult and I had to work very hard to just do
   okay.
